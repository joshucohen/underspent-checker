Underspent Fund Checker

Identify funds that may not be spending at a sustainable rate

What This Tool Does

This tool helps you identify funds that may be underutilized over time. By analyzing multiple years of fund balance and spending data, it calculates average balances, average spending, and a spend rate to highlight funds that may require review or action.

This is particularly useful for financial oversight, stewardship, and identifying areas where funds may be accumulating faster than they are being used.

How to Use

  1. Download the template using the button below
  2. Export your data from your system of record (CRM, ERP, or finance system)
    • You need fund-level data by year
    • Each year should include: fund_id, balance, spend, revenue
  3. Paste your data into the template
    • Do not rename sheets
    • Do not change column names
    • Fill all three sheets (Year1, Year2, Year3)
  4. Upload the completed file
  5. Click Analyze to generate results

Frequently Asked Questions

Where do I get this data?
Export fund-level financial data from your system of record (such as your CRM, ERP, or financial system). You will need balances and spending data by year.

What is “Spend Rate”?
Spend Rate is calculated as Average Spend divided by Average Balance. It helps indicate how actively a fund is being used over time.

What does “underspent” mean?
Funds with low spend rates may indicate that balances are accumulating faster than they are being used. This may require further review depending on institutional policy.

What if my file doesn’t work?
Ensure you are using the provided template exactly as structured: correct sheet names, correct columns, and data in all three sheets.
